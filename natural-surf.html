Hainan Has Real Surf

The waves were always here.
Most travelers just never looked.

Most surfers do not think of China when they think of tropical waves. That is exactly what makes Hainan different. Beyond the wave pool, the island has real ocean surf, seasonal swells, quiet coastlines, long mellow waves, kid-friendly beach conditions, and a side of surfing that still feels undiscovered to most international travelers.

The coastline shifts with the season. Some days are playful and beginner-friendly. Some are gentle enough for kids and first-timers when the conditions are right. Some setups offer especially long, smooth waves built for longboarding. Other days bring more powerful conditions for experienced surfers. The best part is that most travelers do not even know where to look.

The wave pool is the anchor of the trip. The ocean is the surprise — and Hainan has more of it than almost anyone outside the island realises.

The Surf Calendar

Two surf seasons.

Hainan has a regular seasonal surf window and a separate, more unpredictable typhoon swell window. The wave pool gives the trip structure. The ocean adds the surprise.

Variety, Not a Spot Guide

All kinds
of waves.

Hainan’s natural surf is not only about advanced surfers or heavy waves. When the conditions are right, the same coastline can show very different sides.

01 — Longboard

Longboard Waves

Some of Hainan’s natural waves can be long, smooth, and beautifully paced — the kind of waves made for trim, flow, and classic longboard lines. There are some world-class longboard waves here. The WSL has run competitions on this coast.

02 — Family

Family-Friendly Beach Days

When the conditions are soft and safe, there are beach setups that can be great for kids, first-time surfers, and families who want a gentle ocean experience.

03 — Performance

More Serious Surf

When stronger swells line up, Hainan can also show a completely different side — faster, cleaner, more powerful, and better suited for experienced surfers.

Out of Respect for the Coast

What we don’t
