# Package Detail Pages — Structure Guide + Sample Copy

For Surf China Co. (wanning-website) · English copy, premium + lifestyle tone

---

## Part 1 — What I learned from the reference brands

**Soul & Surf** doesn't sell a package. It sells *a day*. Their detail pages walk you through what 6:30am → 9pm actually looks like: the pastel de nata before the dawn session, the 5pm sunset yoga, the communal 7:30 dinner. By the time you finish reading, you already know the rhythm. "Included" feels like a byproduct, not the headline.

**Black Tomato** writes like a travel magazine. Openers are emotional and specific — "To feed your appetites. Follow your passions. Push your limits." They never say "local cuisine." They say "a longevity-focused cooking class and a sake-tasting of Awamori." They close every itinerary with: *"These are simply suggestions. Yours will be tailored, altered, and refined until it matches you completely."*

**Tropicsurf** is the premium surf-travel benchmark. Clear skill-level paths (beginners progress from lagoon → Ninjas reef break), named breaks so surfers feel respected, and relaxation/spa/family equally weighted with waves.

**The three lessons for Surf China Co.:**

1. Show the day, not just the bullet list. A surfer wants to know what 8am feels like.
2. Be specific. "Guided Hainan cultural trips" is forgettable. "Sunrise at the Tianyahaijiao cliffs with breakfast from a 70-year-old Hakka bakery" is not.
3. Treat every package as a starting point, not a menu item. Bake in "we'll tailor this" language.

## Part 2 — The recommended detail page structure

Twelve modules, in this order. Use all of them for your flagship (5–7 day) package. The shorter/longer packages can skip 1–2 modules or collapse them.

### 1. Hero

Full-bleed video or photo (PerfectSwell drone shot works). Overlay:

- **Package name** (big)
- **One-sentence hook** (≤ 14 words) — emotional, not descriptive
- **Chips:** Duration · From $X USD · Group size · Skill level
- **Primary CTA:** "Book Now" + **Secondary CTA:** "Talk to us" / WhatsApp

### 2. The Pitch (3 short paragraphs)

Paragraph 1 — emotional opener. Why someone comes here in the first place.
Paragraph 2 — what the trip actually is, in plain language.
Paragraph 3 — who it's for, who it isn't.

Keep it under 120 words total. This is the scanner's read.

### 3. At-a-Glance (sticky sidebar or horizontal card)

Duration · Group size · Skill level · Best season · Price from · Custom dates available · Languages (EN/CN). Use icons. This is the sheet every serious traveler screenshots.

### 4. A Day in Wanning (the Soul & Surf move)

Sample daily rhythm with times. Not "morning surf, afternoon activities." Instead:

> 6:45am — Coffee and Hainanese crepes at the villa
> 7:30am — First PerfectSwell session, waves to yourself before the crowd
> 10am — Video review with your coach over cold coconut water
> ...

This is the module that sells the trip more than any other. Write it well.

### 5. Sample Itinerary (day by day)

Day 1 to Day X. Each day gets a 2–3 sentence narrative, not bullets. End with a "Customize this" note (Black Tomato style).

### 6. The Surf

Break the surf experience into:
- **The Wave Pool** — PerfectSwell® specs, what it means, session length, wave types
- **Coaching** — who your coach is, video analysis, skill progression
- **Natural Surf** (if included) — Hainan's real breaks with names, wave heights, seasonality

Name things. Surfers trust specificity.

### 7. Where You'll Stay

Short narrative about the property + 3–5 photos + room options (private/shared, upgrade tiers). Mention views, distance to pool, amenities. Don't list; describe.

### 8. Beyond the Water

The cultural/food/nature side. This is where Surf & Discover Hainan *earns its name*. Kill the word "local cuisine." Replace with named experiences: "a seafood dinner at Tanmen fishing harbor, the boats still coming in."

### 9. Who This Is For / Not For

- **This trip is for:** beginners who want fast progression · intermediates who want consistency · couples · solo travelers · small groups
- **This trip probably isn't for:** pros chasing OHH reef · travelers who won't leave the resort

Honesty here builds trust and filters out bad-fit bookings.

### 10. What's Included · What's Not

Two columns. Be exhaustive. Western travelers hate surprises.

Included column: Every single thing. Don't assume they'll guess airport transfer is included.
Not Included column: Flights, visa, travel insurance, alcohol beyond X, personal spending.

### 11. Investment (pricing)

Don't hide the price. "From $X USD per person (twin share)." Show what upgrades cost. Mention single supplement, group discount. End with: "Payment is 30% deposit, balance due 30 days prior."

### 12. FAQ + Good to Know

6–10 real questions. The five that matter most for Wanning:
- Do I need a visa to visit Hainan? (Hainan has 30-day visa-free for many countries — use this!)
- How do I get there?
- How much English is spoken?
- What if the wave pool is down for maintenance?
- What's the weather like in [month]?

### 13. Customize This Trip (the Black Tomato close)

> This is a starting point. Your trip will be shaped around your level, your crew, and what you want out of Hainan. Tell us what you're after — we'll build it.

Followed by a final CTA: **Request a Call** with a WhatsApp / email button.

### FAQ

**Do I need a visa?**
Probably not. Hainan offers 30-day visa-free entry for passport holders from the US, UK, EU, Australia, Canada, and 50+ other countries. We'll walk you through it.

**How do I get to Wanning?**
Fly into Sanya Phoenix International (SYX). We pick you up. Sanya connects to Hong Kong, Singapore, Bangkok, Tokyo, Seoul, and direct from a few European cities seasonally.

**How much English is spoken?**
Your whole trip is run in English by us. Off-property, most locals don't speak English — which is part of why having us makes this trip work.

**What if I've never surfed before?**
Perfect. The wave pool is built for this. Most guests are standing and riding green waves by their third session.

**What if the pool is down for maintenance?**
We'll know months ahead. If a scheduled maintenance window clashes with your dates, we either move dates or lean into more natural surf and exploration days — your call.

**What's the weather like?**
October to April is the season: 22–28°C (72–82°F), warm water, low humidity. May to September is rainier, still surfable, better for avoiding crowds.

## Part 4 — How to adapt this for your other two packages

**The Surf Session (3–4 days)**
- Skip the "Beyond the Water" module entirely, or compress to 2 lines
- "Sample Itinerary" shrinks to 3-4 days and skips the inland / natural surf days
- The pitch leads with "You came for waves. Good." — keep it tight.
- Price from, at-a-glance, and the daily rhythm stay. Those are always the highest-converting sections.

**Surf & Golf (7–9 days)**
- Add a "The Golf" module parallel to "The Surf" — name the course, write about the design, the starter, the greens fee model
- "A Day in Wanning" gets a golf variant ("Golf mornings: 6:30 tee time in the dew, surf in the afternoon")
- "Who This Is For" leans into surfer-golfers and couples where one golfs and one surfs
- Keep the Hainan cultural module — it's what separates this from a Scottsdale package

For both, keep the Black Tomato close at the bottom. Every trip is customizable — that's your edge over the big operators.

## Part 5 — One small piece of advice

The single biggest thing that will move these detail pages from good to great is **photo and video discipline**. Write copy assuming you'll have:

- A 10-second drone clip of PerfectSwell as hero video
- One hero still per day of the sample itinerary
- One face — a guest, mid-stoke — high on the page
- One meal, one scenery, one coaching moment, one wave — in that order — as you scroll

If you don't have the images yet, shoot them before launch. The copy is half the work; the images are the other half.
