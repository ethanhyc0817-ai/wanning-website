Hainan's First People

The island's
original storytellers.

The Li are the indigenous people of Hainan — here thousands of years before the island was mapped, named, or traded. Their language has no written form, so their history lives in what they make: brocade patterns coded with family stories, wooden villages built without nails, songs sung at harvest and at funerals.

You won't find this in a museum queue in Shanghai. The Li live in the mountains and valleys just inland from the surf — a short drive from the wave pool into a part of China most visitors never see.

"A Li woman's wedding blanket tells you where she's from, who her mother was, and which mountain her village belongs to — all before you read a single word."

Li Brocade Weaving

Watch a master weaver work a backstrap loom — a UNESCO-recognized craft with patterns passed mother to daughter for a thousand years.

02 — Place

Ancient Villages

Walk through stilted wooden houses and boat-shaped roofs in a restored Li settlement — architecture designed for monsoons long before concrete reached the island.

A sit-down dinner with a live performance — bamboo-pole dancing, fire shows, and Li folk songs. Part feast, part concert, part history lesson.
