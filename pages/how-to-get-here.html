The Short Version

Fly to Hainan.
We’ll do the rest.

Most people picture Hainan as somewhere obscure. It’s not. It’s China’s southernmost province — a tropical island the size of Belgium — with two international airports, a high-speed rail loop, and direct flights from Moscow, London, Singapore, Bangkok, Seoul, and dozens of other hubs.

You book one flight to either Haikou (HAK) or Sanya (SYX). Our driver is waiting at arrivals with your name on a sign. From there it’s a one- to two-hour drive down the coast in a private business van, and you’re standing at the pool. No domestic transfers, no rental cars, no figuring out a Chinese train app at midnight.

“One international flight, one driver, one drive. That’s the whole journey — and most of it has an ocean view.”
Step 1 · Fly Into Hainan

Two international airports.
Pick whichever flight works.

Hainan has two full international airports on opposite ends of the island. Haikou is the bigger international hub; Sanya is the closer one to the Wave Pool. Both work — we transfer from either.

Where People Fly From

Direct routes from
four corners of the world.

The flight options below are the direct, non-stop routes operating year-round or seasonally. If you don’t see your city, almost any major Asian hub (Hong Kong, Singapore, Bangkok, Seoul) is a one-stop connection.

01 — Europe

From Europe

~10–11 hour flight
  • London (LHR) → Haikou, year-round
  • Brussels (BRU) → Haikou, twice weekly
  • Other cities: 1 stop via London, Frankfurt, or Doha
02 — North America

From the US & Canada

~17–20 hours total · 1 stop
  • West Coast (LAX, SFO, SEA, YVR) via Hong Kong, Seoul, or Tokyo
  • East Coast (JFK, EWR, BOS, YYZ) via Hong Kong, Beijing, or Shanghai
  • One same-day onward flight to Haikou or Sanya — we book the connection
03 — Russia & CIS

From Russia

~9–10 hour flight
  • Moscow (SVO) → Sanya, year-round
  • Moscow (SVO) → Haikou, year-round
  • Seasonal charters from St. Petersburg, Novosibirsk
04 — Southeast Asia

From SE Asia

~3–5 hour flight
  • Singapore (SIN) → Haikou, daily
  • Bangkok (BKK) → Haikou, year-round
  • Kuala Lumpur, Hanoi, Ho Chi Minh, Kota Kinabalu
05 — East Asia

From Korea & Japan

~3–4 hour flight
  • Seoul (ICN) → Haikou & Sanya, multiple weekly
  • Tokyo, Osaka, Taipei via Shanghai or Hong Kong
  • Hong Kong is the most reliable hub for any onward connection
06 — Australia

From Australia

~12–14 hours total · 1 stop
  • Sydney / Melbourne / Perth via Hong Kong, Singapore, or Guangzhou
  • Sydney→HKG→HAK is the smoothest routing
  • One same-day connection — we book it for you
Step 2 · The Airport Pickup

Land. Walk out.
Find your name on a sign.

The moment you exit customs, an English-speaking driver from our team is waiting in the arrivals hall. You don’t need to find a taxi, queue at a counter, or download an app. From the gate to the resort is one continuous handoff.

01

You send us your flight

Once your ticket is booked, you forward us the e-ticket. We track your flight in real time — if it’s delayed, our driver shifts with it. No phone calls needed from your end.

02

Driver waiting at arrivals

An English-speaking driver from our team meets you in the arrivals hall holding a sign with your name. WhatsApp number is shared in advance, so if anything is unclear, you have a real person to call.

03

Private business van

Modern Mercedes / Buick business van (MPV) with Wi-Fi, bottled water, and space for boards and luggage. Up to 6 passengers per vehicle — bigger groups get a second car.

04

The drive to the wave

From Sanya it’s about 60 minutes up the coast. From Haikou, around 2 hours down the east coast. Either way, most of it is highway with the ocean on one side and tropical mountains on the other.

05

Check-in, then surf

Driver drops you at the resort, your trip coordinator meets you in the lobby with a cold towel and the schedule for the next day. If you arrive with energy, the pool is a 5-minute walk.

FAQ

The questions we get most.

Which airport should I fly into — Haikou or Sanya?

Whichever has the better flight for you. Sanya (SYX) is closer to the resort (1-hour drive vs. 2 hours from Haikou), but Haikou (HAK) has more international routes — especially from Europe. Send us your departure city and we’ll tell you the best option.

Is the airport transfer included?

Yes. Every package includes round-trip private transfers between either airport and the resort. There’s nothing extra to book or pay for the drive.

What if my flight is delayed or arrives late at night?

We track your flight live. If you’re delayed, the driver shifts with the new arrival time — no need to call us. Late-night arrivals are fine; the resort accepts check-ins 24/7.

What if I bring my own surfboard?

No problem. Our business vans are sized for boards and bags. If you’re traveling with multiple boards or a big group, just let us know in advance and we’ll size the vehicle accordingly.

Do I need to speak Chinese?

No. Your driver, trip coordinator, and resort staff all speak English. WhatsApp is the main channel for communication before, during, and after your stay.

Can I add a stopover in Sanya or Haikou?

Absolutely. A lot of guests spend a night in Sanya at the start or end of the trip — beach hotels, seafood, the city. We can arrange the extra night and the transfer split.

What if I’m flying from the US, Canada, or Australia — no direct flights?

One stop is all it takes. From North America, the smoothest routings go through Hong Kong, Seoul (ICN), or Tokyo on the West Coast, and through Hong Kong, Beijing, or Shanghai on the East Coast. From Australia, Hong Kong or Singapore is the cleanest. All of these hubs have multiple onward flights to Haikou or Sanya every day — we’ll help you pick the connection.
