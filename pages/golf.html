Hainan's Quiet Reputation

Before the surf,
there was the swing.

Hainan has been China's premier golf destination for more than twenty years — long before anyone was talking about surfing here. Volcanic terrain in the north, ocean coastlines in the east and south, and within an hour's drive you can tee off in three completely different landscapes.

The result is a quiet collection of world-class courses: China's #1 and #2 ranked courses, a Golf Digest World Top 100 layout, and one of Asia's most exclusive private clubs — all on a single tropical island. The HSBC Champions, the Omega Mission Hills World Cup, the Volvo China Open: if you watch professional golf, you've almost certainly watched a tournament on a Hainan fairway without knowing where you were looking.

01 · Haikou · #1 in China

Mission Hills Blackstone

Schmidt-Curley · 2009 · Volcanic lava-rock

Mission Hills Haikou is the world's largest golf complex — ten championship courses carved out of black volcanic lava rock, all within a single resort. Blackstone is its featured course: a dramatic layout through volcanic rock and lush tropical scenery, and the venue where Tiger Woods and Rory McIlroy went head-to-head in 2013. A true bucket-list destination for golfers who want real history, world-class design, and a setting you don't find anywhere else.

02 · Wanning · #2 in China

The Dunes at Shenzhou

Tom Weiskopf & Phil Smith · 2014 · Coastal links · Par 72

A coastal links shaped by sand dunes, ocean wind, and the South China Sea. Few golf courses in China have a setting like this — built along the coast with natural sand dunes, dramatic bunkers, and wide-open ocean views, it delivers a true links experience with a tropical island edge. Raw, cinematic, and deeply connected to the landscape. Just 30 minutes from the surf pool.

And there's more of it than most people expect. The Dunes is really two courses in one — 36 holes across the East and West layouts, threaded along the same wild stretch of coast. Ask the golfers we've sent: the West is the runaway favorite, and the round we point you to first.

03 · Sanya · Headland Routing

Lu Hui Tou Golf Club

Nelson & Haworth · 2009 · 7,310 yds · Par 72

Sanya's headland course — sea on one side, mountain on the other. Eighteen holes draped across the Lu Hui Tou peninsula, between the South China Sea and the green mountain ridge. Nelson & Haworth routed the course with elevation changes that give every approach a different angle. Pair the round with an evening at Rosewood Sanya and the polished island day arranges itself.
